About

Why this exists

American elections aren't run by one set of rules — they're run by fifty-one of them. Each state decides who draws its district maps, how people register, how money flows into campaigns, and how results get checked. Those choices quietly shape every election that follows.

This tool turns that scattered landscape into something you can actually read. We grade each state's democratic health, map where reform has the most room to move, and track the news that nudges those scores up or down — so you can see not just where things stand, but where to push.

The scores and headlines shown here are illustrative sample data for a prototype. The structure is built to plug into live indices, legislative trackers, and original reporting.

How to read the index

  • Map Color-codes every state by health score. Hover for a snapshot, click for the full profile.
  • Score A 0–100 composite of access, map fairness, finance transparency, security, and participation.
  • State Two panels — the story in words on the left, the numbers and charts on the right.
  • News Each item shows the score change it triggers, with a link to our analysis.
